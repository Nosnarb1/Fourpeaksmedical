MAT & Suboxone

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) is an evidence-based, FDA-approved approach to treating opioid use disorder. It uses medications like Suboxone (buprenorphine/naloxone) to reduce cravings and withdrawal symptoms, making it easier to focus on recovery and daily life.

MAT is not "replacing one drug with another" — it is a medical treatment, just like medication for any other chronic condition. At Four Peaks Medical, MAT patients receive the same compassionate, structured care as all our patients.

Suboxone is a brand name for buprenorphine/naloxone — the most commonly used medication in MAT for opioid use disorder. It works by binding to opioid receptors in the brain to reduce withdrawal and cravings without producing a significant "high."

Whether it is right for you depends on your individual situation. Call our office and we can discuss your case and help you understand your options.

No. Opioid use disorder is a medical condition. Seeking help is not a weakness — it is the right thing to do. At Four Peaks Medical, MAT patients are treated with the same dignity and respect as every other patient. Our practice was built around this belief.
